Omar Vizquel

11-time old glove winning shortstop

Omar Vizquel making a diving stop at shortstop for the Cleveland Indians

Here is a timeline of Omar's life and accomplishments

  • April 24, 1967 - Omar Enrique Vizquel Gonzalez is born in Caracas, Venezuela
  • 1977 - Omar Vizquel was instrumental in Venezuela winning the Little League World Series
  • April 3, 1989 - Made Major League debut for the Seattle Mariners
  • 1993 - Won first of 11 Gold Gloves
  • December 20, 1993 - Traded to the Cleveland Indians
  • 1994 - Helped the Indians to their first World Series appearance since 1954
  • October 3rd. 2012 - Played final game, retiring after 24 seasons in Major League baseball

One of the greatest fielding shortstops of all-time, Omar Vizquel spent 24 years in the major leagues, collecting 2,877 hits and stealing 404 bases. An 11-time Gold Glover and three-time All-Star, Vizquel helped lead the Cleveland Indians to six playoff berths and two World Series. And no one in baseball history has played more games at shortstop (2,709).
